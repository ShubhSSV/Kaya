Plant Information


Plant Name:Kaya


Benefits: The Kaya tree (Millettia pinnata), also known as the Pongam tree, is renowned for its wide range of benefits. Its seeds contain oil rich in fatty acids, commonly used as biofuel due to its environmentally friendly properties. Kaya leaves and bark have medicinal properties, widely used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory and antibacterial effects. These parts of the plant are helpful for managing skin conditions, soothing rheumatic pain, and relieving digestive issues. Furthermore, the Kaya tree contributes to soil fertility through nitrogen fixation, enhancing soil quality and making it valuable in sustainable agriculture.



Season: The Kaya tree typically flowers in the late spring and early summer, making this period the most visually appealing season for the plant. During this time, the tree produces small, purple, pea-like flowers that emit a mild fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The flowering period also marks the onset of seed production, with seeds ripening toward the end of summer and early autumn. In regions with warmer climates, some Kaya trees may flower and bear seeds more consistently year-round, though they are most prolific in these seasonal windows.



Region: Indigenous to the Indian subcontinent and parts of Southeast Asia, the Kaya tree thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It is especially prevalent in countries like India, Bangladesh, Myanmar, and Thailand. Over time, the Kaya tree has been introduced to various regions outside Asia, such as Australia and parts of Africa, due to its adaptability to different soil types, including saline and degraded soils. Its resilience to drought and varying soil conditions makes it a favored choice for reforestation and erosion control efforts in diverse environments.



Age: The Kaya tree has a long lifespan, often reaching up to 100 years or more. It typically matures in 4 to 5 years, after which it begins to flower and produce seeds. However, its full height and canopy spread, which can extend to around 15 to 25 meters, are achieved over several decades. With proper care, the Kaya tree can continue to thrive and produce seeds well into old age, making it a sustainable resource for generations.
